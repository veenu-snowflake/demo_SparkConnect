SLIDE 6 - BUILDING ON SPARK CONNECT

Now, to understand what we built, let me give you a little background. Because we did not just make up something proprietary. We built on top of something the Spark community already created.

In Spark 3.4, the Apache Spark project introduced something called Spark Connect. And what Spark Connect did was pretty clever. It took the old monolithic Spark architecture and split it into a clean client-server model.

On the left, you have the client side. Your applications, your IDEs, your notebooks, your programming languages. This is where you write code. This part stays exactly the same.

On the right, you have the server side. The optimizer, the scheduler, the execution engine. This is where the actual processing happens.

And the two talk to each other through a thin API layer called the Spark Connect API.

Now here is the breakthrough. Because of this clean separation, you can swap out the server. You do not have to use a Spark cluster as the backend anymore.

And that is exactly what we did.

SLIDE 13 - SNOWPARK CONNECT: HOW IT WORKS UNDER THE HOOD

Okay, this is the architecture slide. Let me walk you through the six steps.

Step one. Your client runs standard open source Spark Connect. Nothing proprietary. Standard PySpark.

Step two. Spark Connect sends the Apache Spark query plan to Snowflake. This is an unresolved logical plan, sent over gRPC. Just like it would send to any Spark server.

Step three. Snowpark Connect interprets that plan. It has a SparkSQL parser, semantic reasoning, and metadata management built in.

Step four. It optimizes the plan for Snowflake. Query simplification, caching, all the smart stuff that makes it fast.

Step five. It hands the execution off to Snowpark.

Step six. Snowpark runs the optimized query on Snowflake's vectorized engine.

So internally, we receive the code from your Jupyter notebook or VS Code, it comes in through the Spark Connect API, and on our end we interpret it and convert it into an optimized SQL query plan. That is the beauty of Snowpark Connect. The power of the Snowflake engine comes together with the familiarity of the Spark API.

And your data can live in Snowflake tables, internally or externally managed Iceberg tables, or cloud object storage. Snowflake speaks Spark so you do not have to speak Snowflake.
